## Getting Started

### Q: What is the Day Trading Orchestrator?
**A:** The Day Trading Orchestrator is a comprehensive trading platform that automates trading across multiple brokers using AI-powered strategies. It provides real-time market analysis, automated order execution, risk management, and a unified interface for managing all your trading activities.

### Q: Do I need trading experience to use this platform?
**A:** No, the platform is designed for all experience levels. We provide:
- Interactive tutorials for beginners
- Advanced features for experienced traders
- Automated strategies that require minimal manual intervention
- Comprehensive risk management tools

### Q: What does "orchestrator" mean in this context?
**A:** The orchestrator coordinates multiple trading components:
- Multiple broker connections
- Various trading strategies
- Real-time market data feeds
- Risk management systems
- Order execution and tracking
- Portfolio management

### Q: How quickly can I start trading?
**A:** You can be trading within 30 minutes:
1. Create an account (5 minutes)
2. Connect your first broker (10 minutes)
3. Configure basic settings (10 minutes)
4. Start with paper trading (5 minutes)

### Q: What is paper trading?
**A:** Paper trading allows you to practice trading with virtual money in real market conditions. It's the safest way to learn and test strategies without risking actual capital. All features work identically to live trading.

### Q: Can I use this for different types of trading?
**A:** Yes, the platform supports:
- Day trading (buy and sell within the same day)
- Swing trading (positions held for days/weeks)
- Scalping (rapid small profits)
- Position trading (long-term holdings)
- Algorithmic trading (fully automated)

### Q: What is a trading strategy?
**A:** A trading strategy is a set of rules that determine:
- When to enter a trade
- When to exit a trade
- How much to invest (position sizing)
- How to manage risk

### Q: Do I need to create my own strategies?
**A:** No, we provide:
- Pre-built strategies ready to use
- Customizable parameters for each strategy
- Strategy builder for advanced users
- Community strategy sharing

### Q: How do I choose the right strategy?
**A:** Consider these factors:
- **Your experience level**
- **Time available for trading**
- **Risk tolerance**
- **Market conditions you prefer**
- **Capital allocation**

Our strategy recommendation engine can help based on your profile.

### Q: Can I run multiple strategies simultaneously?
**A:** Yes! You can:
- Run unlimited strategies simultaneously
- Assign different capital allocations to each
- Set strategy-specific risk limits
- Monitor individual strategy performance

### Q: What are strategy parameters?
**A:** Parameters are settings you can adjust for each strategy:
- **Moving Average Periods:** How many days to average
- **Risk Percentage:** How much to risk per trade
- **Stop Loss Distance:** When to exit losing trades
- **Take Profit Levels:** When to exit winning trades

### Q: How do I know if a strategy is working?
**A:** Monitor these metrics:
- **Win Rate:** Percentage of profitable trades
- **Profit Factor:** Gross profit divided by gross loss
- **Sharpe Ratio:** Risk-adjusted returns
- **Maximum Drawdown:** Largest losing streak

### Q: Can I backtest strategies?
**A:** Yes! Backtesting allows you to:
- Test strategies with historical data
- See how they would have performed
- Optimize parameters before going live
- Compare different strategies

## Risk Management

### Q: What is risk management?
**A:** Risk management protects your capital by:
- Limiting how much you can lose per trade
- Diversifying across different strategies
- Setting maximum portfolio exposure
- Using stop losses and position sizing

### Q: What is position sizing?
**A:** Position sizing determines how much to invest in each trade:
- Based on your risk tolerance
- Calculated using stop loss distance
- Ensures you never risk too much on one trade
- Follows the 1% rule (never risk more than 1% per trade)

### Q: What is a stop loss?
**A:** A stop loss automatically exits a trade when it loses a certain amount:
- Protects you from large losses
- Based on technical levels or percentage
- Can be fixed or trailing
- Essential for risk management

### Q: How do I set risk limits?
**A:** You can set limits in the Risk Management section:
- **Per Trade:** Maximum risk per individual trade
- **Per Day:** Maximum loss allowed per day
- **Per Strategy:** Limits for each strategy
- **Portfolio:** Maximum total portfolio risk

### Q: What are circuit breakers?
**A:** Circuit breakers automatically stop trading when:
- Losses exceed your daily limit
- Market volatility is extreme
- Technical issues are detected
- Security threats are identified

### Q: Can I trade on margin?
**A:** Yes, if your broker supports it:
- Check your broker's margin requirements
- Set conservative margin usage limits
- Monitor margin levels in real-time
- Understand the risks of leverage

### Q: What happens if I lose money?
**A:** The platform will:
- Automatically close losing positions if stop losses are hit
- Stop trading if daily loss limits are reached
- Send alerts when approaching risk limits
- Provide detailed loss analysis

### Q: How do I diversify my portfolio?
**A:** Diversification reduces risk by:
- Using multiple trading strategies
- Trading different asset classes
- Spreading risk across time periods
- Avoiding highly correlated positions

## Performance and Optimization

### Q: How can I improve my trading performance?
**A:** Focus on these areas:
- **Stick to your strategy:** Don't deviate from proven methods
- **Manage risk properly:** Never risk more than you can afford to lose
- **Keep learning:** Markets evolve, so should you
- **Track everything:** Maintain a trading journal
- **Be patient:** Good trading takes time to develop

### Q: What is a good win rate?
**A:** Win rates vary by strategy:
- **Day trading:** 50-60% is excellent
- **Swing trading:** 60-70% is good
- **Trend following:** 40-50% is normal
- **Remember:** High win rates can still be unprofitable if losses are large

### Q: How long does it take to become profitable?
**A:** Timeline varies greatly:
- **Paper trading:** Practice for 2-3 months minimum
- **Small live trades:** Start with minimal amounts
- **Consistent profitability:** Usually 6-12 months
- **Mastery:** 2-3 years of dedicated practice

### Q: Why am I losing money?
**A:** Common reasons:
- **Poor risk management:** Risking too much per trade
- **Emotional trading:** Making impulsive decisions
- **Overtrading:** Too many trades with high costs
- **Market conditions:** Markets can be unpredictable
- **Strategy mismatch:** Strategy doesn't fit your style

### Q: How do I optimize my strategies?
**A:** Optimization tips:
- **Backtest thoroughly:** Test on multiple time periods
- **Avoid overfitting:** Don't optimize too much for past data
- **Walk forward:** Test on out-of-sample data
- **Paper trade first:** Test before going live
- **Start small:** Begin with minimal position sizes

### Q: What is a good risk-reward ratio?
**A:** Aim for positive expectancy:
- **Minimum ratio:** 1:1 (break even on 50% win rate)
- **Good ratio:** 2:1 (profit on 34% win rate)
- **Excellent ratio:** 3:1 (profit on 25% win rate)
- **Remember:** Higher ratios mean fewer trades
